Global Reach

Each time you request to access a webpage over the global network known as the internet, your request is broken down into packets that are routed and transmitted across the network. These packets travel between routers and servers, often located in different places, to reach their destination.
In your device's terminal, you can type the commands 'ping example.com' or 'tracert example.com' to obtain information about packet transmission speed and location. Select a domain below and press 'enter' to run these commands. You will be able to see the information retreived as you would in the terminal along with the various locations that your packets have reached. (Be patient as the request is being processed).
